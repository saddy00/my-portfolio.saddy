Research

Applications of linear algebra in the field of a digital image processing

San Jacinto College Mathematics Department · Coordinator: Abigail Payne

I researched how topics in linear algebra are applied to digital image processing. In the research, I learned how an image can be represented as a matrix, how matrix operations and matrix transformations can be performed on an image. I worked on two specific computations illustrating how topics from the course can be applied to digital image processing.

January 2020 – May 2020

Finding numerical solutions for the differential equations using Euler’s and Runge-Kutta methods

San Jacinto College Mathematics Department · Coordinator: Ali Elhaj

My research is based on finding a numerical solution for the differential equations using the following methods: Euler Method and Runge-Kutta Method Order 4. I write a computer program to generate the values and visualize both methods. Part of the research required showing the efficiency of both methods by comparing the algebraic method's solutions.

January 2020 – Present

Muon Identification at Muon Endcap Sub-detector of CMS Detector

San Jacinto College Physics Department · Coordinator: Radia Redjimi

My study was about the general-purpose detector, Compact Muon Solenoid, which detects and measures muons. Muons are particles much heavier than electrons and are created by the collision of cosmic rays. This project focused on studying the electronics used to detect these particles and the properties used to identify them. I analyzed the principle, implementation, and performance of a muon identification at the cathode strip chamber sub-detector. We work on building the school’s very first particle detector for the cosmic ray observatory.
